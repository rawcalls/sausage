Directions

  1. Prepare Ingredients:
    • Chill ground beef chuck and pork butt to 35–40°F (2–4°C).
    • Soak muslin casings in lukewarm water for 1–2 hours to soften. Rinse well and keep damp.
    • Dissolve T-SPX starter culture in 1/4 cup distilled water and let sit for 10 minutes to activate.
  2. Mix Seasonings and Meat:
    • Combine ground beef chuck and pork butt in a large, chilled bowl.
    • Add salt, dextrose, pickling spice, black pepper, minced garlic, ground coriander, whole mustard seed, sodium erythorbate, and Cure #1. Mix thoroughly for 1–2 minutes.
    • Add T-SPX slurry and remaining distilled water. Mix until sticky and cohesive (1–2 minutes).
  3. Stuff Sausages:
    • Load the sausage stuffer with the mixture, avoiding air pockets.
    • Stuff into muslin casings, forming 10–12 inch logs. Tie ends with butcher’s twine. Prick air bubbles.
  4. Fermentation During Cold Smoking:
    • Hang sausages in a smoker at 75°F (24°C) with 80–85% humidity. Apply light smoke (e.g., hickory or applewood) for 72 hours.
    • T-SPX and dextrose will ferment, lowering pH to ~5.0. Check pH after 72 hours.
  5. Hot Smoking:
    • Increase smoker temperature gradually to 160°F (71°C), maintaining light smoke and 70–80% humidity.
    • Smoke until internal temperature reaches 148°F (64°C), about 3–5 hours. Verify pH remains ~5.0.
  6. Cooling:
    • Place sausages in an ice-water bath to cool below 120°F (49°C). Pat dry and rest for 1–2 hours.
  7. Storage:
    • Remove muslin casings or leave on. Vacuum-seal or wrap tightly. Refrigerate at 35–40°F (2–4°C) for up to 2 weeks or freeze for 6 months.

Notes

  • Fat Content: Yields 27.5% total fat for better texture.
  • T-SPX: Mimics Nebraska’s wild fermentation.
  • Dextrose: Adjust to 0.6% if pH drops below 4.8, or 0.8% if above 5.0.
  • Safety: Cure #1 prevents botulism. Hot smoking to 148°F is safe, though 150°F is more conservative.
  • Nebraska Climate: Use a humidifier to maintain 80–85% humidity during cold smoking.
