Instructions

  1. Prepare Ingredients:
    • Chill ground beef chuck and pork butt to 35–40°F (2–4°C) to ensure safety and ease of mixing.
    • Soak muslin casings in lukewarm water for 1–2 hours to soften, ensuring they’re pliable for stuffing and smoke penetration. Rinse well and keep damp until use.
    • Dissolve T-SPX starter culture (adjusted quantity) in 1/4 cup distilled water and let sit for 10 minutes to activate.
  2. Mix Seasonings and Meat:
  3. Stuff Sausages:
    • Load the sausage stuffer with the meat mixture, avoiding air pockets.
    • Stuff into muslin casings, forming 10–12 inch logs or as desired (muslin casings are typically larger, often 2–3 inches in diameter). Tie ends securely with butcher’s twine, ensuring a tight pack to prevent air gaps. Prick any air bubbles with a sterile pin.
  4. Fermentation During Cold Smoking:
    • Hang sausages in a smoker set to 75°F (24°C) with 80–85% relative humidity. Apply light, consistent smoke using hardwood (e.g., hickory or applewood) for 60 hours.
    • The T-SPX culture and dextrose will ferment slowly, lowering the pH to ~5.0–5.1. Ensure good airflow to support fermentation and smoke penetration through the muslin casings.
    • After 60 hours, check pH with a meter or test strips to confirm it’s near 5.0–5.1.
  5. Hot Smoking:
    • Gradually increase smoker temperature by 10°F per hour, starting from 75°F to 160°F (71°C), maintaining light smoke and 70–80% humidity to prevent case hardening.
    • Smoke until the internal temperature reaches 148°F (64°C), typically 8–9 hours (75°F to 160°F = 85°F, at 10°F/hour). Monitor with a probe thermometer inserted through the muslin casing.
    • Verify pH post-hot smoking is ~5.0. If slightly above, extend hot smoking at 148°F for 1–2 hours and recheck.
  6. Cooling:
    • Immediately place sausages in an ice-water bath to cool below 120°F (49°C) to stop cooking and ensure food safety. Muslin casings can be left on during cooling.
    • Pat dry and let rest at room temperature for 1–2 hours to stabilize.
  7. Storage:
    • Remove muslin casings after cooling, or leave on if preferred for storage (they can be peeled off before consumption). Vacuum-seal or wrap tightly and refrigerate at 35–40°F (2–4°C) for up to 2 weeks or freeze for up to 6 months.

Notes

  • Fat Content: Yields 27.5% total fat for better texture.
  • T-SPX: Mimics Nebraska’s wild fermentation.
  • Dextrose: Adjust to 0.6% if pH drops below 4.8, or 0.8% if above 5.0.
  • Safety: Cure #1 prevents botulism. Hot smoking to 148°F is safe, though 150°F is more conservative.
  • Nebraska Climate: Use a humidifier to maintain 80–85% humidity during cold smoking.
